Crisis and Emergency Contexts

Understanding the Context

Children and families can be profoundly impacted by crisis situations, natural disasters, family violence, and displacement, which upset the feeling of safety and stability. Children are usually left traumatized, confused, and requiring immediate aid after the occurrence of natural disasters such as floods, bushfires, and earthquakes (Spencer & Thompson, 2024).

Domestic violence may result in emotional abuse, anxiety, and inability to establish trust due to the sense of insecurity and fear of another simple carelessness. Divorce may result in children belonging to war, domestic violence, natural disasters causing loss and insecurity in children owing to being separated with their families or communities (Malema et al., 2023).

Trauma is very common to children in such situations, and the emotional impact it has upon them may impact their development and social skills. Pre-school is also important in enabling children to cope with trauma, give them a sense of normalcy, and restore emotional strength. During both, the education community can assist the children by providing safe and nurturing environments that will aid the recovery and emotional control process (Sun et al., 2024).

Impact on Children and Families

When children are exposed to such emergencies as natural disasters or family violence, displacement it can have a severe impact on the development process. These activities can be very stressful, which may result in the inability to accommodate new routines and the sense of security. The children can feel frightened, lost, and perplexed and this may impede their behavioral and emotional control (Koplewicz et al., 2006).

The result of the trauma that occurs in such a situation can be a long lasting mental distress which may be anxiety, depression or PTSD. Other problems that children exposed to family violence experience are attachment problems and development of relationships both at home and in school. Displaced or shelter children also tend to lose their sense of community and place thus adding to the sense of insecurity (NEI et al., 1992).

Such occurrences complicate socialization because kids can withdraw, may be unable to trust other people, or engage in anti-social behavior as a result of unaddressed emotional issues. The early childhood educators play a key role in providing the stability, consistency, and emotional support to assist the youngsters in working through these hurdles.

Social Policy and Australian Responses

Australia has instituted diverse national and state policies that would help in responding to crises and the effects on children and families. The government responds to the problem of natural disasters by organizing relief activities using agencies like Australian Red Cross who offer much needed relief to the affected communities including psychological support to the children (Australian Red Cross, 2020).

The National Plan to Reduce Violence against Women and their Children (Australian Government, 2025) is aimed at the response to the family violence and provision of services and support to both children and families affected by the violence (safe housing, provision of mental health services etc.). Also, the policies on natural-disaster-induced displacement, domestic violence, or war encompass policies on residential placement and reintegration of children and families.

Recent statistics reveal that 2 out of every 5 children have witnessed or heard about the family violence (Catholiccare, 2025) and an number of about 1.4 million people including children are affected by the natural calamities occurring in Australia each year (UNICEF, 2025b). These policies are critical in dealing with the consequences of such crisis.

Strategies for Practice

  • Crisis Management Plans: Prepare classroom plans which are clear and articulate in mitigating such situations so that the children are safe and sound during crises.
  • Trauma-Informed Pedagogy: Enact teaching policies that will recognize and accommodate the trauma of children so that they will feel safe and understood at the classroom.
  • Disaster Recovery Programs: Provide coping mechanisms to the kids after the natural disasters including relaxation methods, expression activities and community restoration practices.
  • Peer Support Networks: Promote the establishment of good direction of relationships among children, helping them have the feeling of solidarity and emotional support.
  • Family Reintegration Programs: Support relations between family members where there is domestic violence or displacement and assist the family members to recreate a stable and nurturing environment to the children.

Community and Professional Partnerships

Some of these organizations important towards assisting children and families in crisis situations are:

  • Red Cross – Provides emergency relief and psychological support for children and families affected by natural disasters and displacement (Redcross, 2025a).
  • The Salvation Army – Offers housing, counseling, and support services to families impacted by domestic violence and displacement.
  • Australian Red Cross – Offers assistance in times of crisis, focusing on children's emotional wellbeing and support services for families (Redcross, 2025b).
  • Kids Helpline – Provides counseling and support for children affected by crisis situations such as family violence and trauma (Kidshelpline, 2025).
  • Headspace – Provides mental health support and counseling for young people, helping them recover from trauma and crisis-related stress (Headspace, 2025).
