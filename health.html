Health and Wellbeing Contexts

Understanding the Context

The mental disorder, drugs, and mental trauma of the parent are major problems that affect children emotionally and psychologically (Downey & Crummy, 2022). The emotional needs of a child are impeded by parental mental illness such as anxiety, and depression that may cause neglect or erratic care, thus interfering with emotional needs of a child.

The family background of substance abuse generates a chaotic situation as children are subjected to ignorance, emotional abuse, and unsafe circumstances. Experiences of abuse, family violence or loss could deeply wound the emotional wellbeing of a child resulting in emotional regulation and behavior incompetence (Downey & Crummy, 2022).

Children belonging to such families can be emotionally, socially, and learning impaired. Increased anxiety and fear and depression can interfere with the capacity to attend to school or build good relationships. Early childhood works up in establishing a favorable environment in enabling children to deal and triumph in facing the challenges related to their situation, creating a feeling of safety and emotional development (UNICEF, 2025a).

Impact on Children and Families

Mentally ill, substance abusing or traumatized children are highly vulnerable to emotional and behavioral difficulties. The mental illness of the parents may take the form of neglect and emotional instability that destabilizes the emotional regulation of children and their relationships with other people. Inconsistent care giving may result in the appearance of such children as withdrawn, anxious, and having anger and trusting problems (Kganyago Mphaphuli, 2023).

Drug abuse is a worsening factor since this leads to an unsafe environment where kids are either ignored or emotionally abused. They might feel more stressed and anxious, develop the difficulties related to trusting people and form secure bonds with others (Goldberg & Blaauw, 2019).

Trauma, either abuse or family violence, may lead to mental illnesses such as anxiety, depression or PTSD. Attachment issues can also be found among children as they find it difficult to form relationships with other children and their parents. The challenges can commonly interfere with the performance of attendees at these schools, since they can be unable to focus or develop certain behavioral problems or the inability to establish social connections with other individuals (Ore-ofe Loveth Oluwajobi et al., 2024).

Social Policy and Australian Responses

Australia has various policies to cover the needs of children who are exposed to mental illness and substance abuse or trauma. Headspace is a national youth mental health foundation, which offers early intervention services as it provides counseling, mental health support and resources that address child and young people experiencing mental health issues or trauma.

The other essential contribution is the National Disability Insurance Scheme (NDIS) which plays a fundamental role especially among the children who may be challenged by mental health issues or developmental limitations following trauma or exposure to substances. The NDIS provides tailored care and resources to assist with managing these barriers to assist families (WWDA, 2019).

The recent statistics indicate that 21.5% Australians will experience a mental problem in a single year and most of them are parents (ABS.GOV, 2023). This underscores the importance of such services as Headspace and the NDIS that are essential in reducing the effects of parental health challenges on the development of children as they also provide the support and nurture the youngsters require.

Strategies for Practice

  • Trauma Informed Care: Adopt the implementing of practices that focus on ensuring children are first safe, and able to regulate their emotions, and promote healing by developing a contained, predictable environment.
  • Mindfulness Practices: Present practices on mindfulness including deep breathing, guided imagery, and relaxation skills to enable children to cope up with stress and have orderly emotions.
  • Parental Mental Health Support: Link families to counseling or mental health providers so they get help with parental mental health problems and children be able to access the support they require at home.
  • Secure Attachments Strategies: Promote secure attachments to achieve the following objectives; help the child to develop regular routines; demonstrate emotional availability; and give sensitive parenting.
  • Health Professional Collaboration: Join efforts with psychologist, therapists or any mental National Development Council professionals to offer more specific assistance to a child who has experienced a traumatic event or who is struggling with some type of mental health issue.

Community and Professional Partnerships

There are numerous organizations, that offer important help to children and families with mental illness, substance abuse, and trauma:

  • Beyond Blue – Provides mental health resources for children, parents, and educators, focusing on anxiety, depression, and emotional wellbeing (Beyondblue, 2025).
  • Headspace – Offers mental health services for young people, including counseling, therapy, and mental health education (Headspace, n.d.).
  • Kids Helpline – Provides confidential counseling for children and young people dealing with mental health, family issues, or trauma.
  • The Australian Psychological Society – Offers resources and referrals for psychological services for children and families (APS, 2025).
  • The Salvation Army – Provides family support services, including assistance for families affected by substance abuse and trauma (Salvationarmy, 2025).
